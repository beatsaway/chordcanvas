# Random by Key — Styles reference (for your sister)

Below, **“chords we choose”** = which scale degrees (roots) can appear.  
**“Coloring”** = which qualities/extensions each of those roots is allowed to have (e.g. triad, maj7, m7, add9).

The app supports **37 chord types**, including:  
triads (major, minor, dim, aug), 7ths (maj7, m7, 7, dim7, mMaj7, m7b5), 6ths (6, m6), 9ths (maj9, m9, 9, 7b9, 7#9), 11ths (maj11, m11, 11), 13ths (maj13, m13, 13), add chords (add2, add4, add9, add11, add13), 6/9, sus2, sus4, 7sus4, 9sus4, 7b5, 7#5, 7#11, 9#11.

All of this is in **one key** (e.g. C major). Roman numerals = scale degrees in that key (I = root, ii = 2nd, etc.).

---

## 1. **Current (Diatonic)**

**Chords we choose:** I, ii, iii or III, IV or iv, V, vi, vii° (all 7 degrees, with iii/III and IV/iv options).

**Coloring for each:**
- **I:**   triad, maj7  
- **ii:**  m, m7  
- **iii:** m, m7  **or**  major, maj7 (III)  
- **IV:**  major, maj7  **or**  m, mMaj7 (iv)  
- **V:**   major, 7 (dominant)  
- **vi:**  m, m7  
- **vii°:** dim, m7b5  

**Rule:** 33% chance to use the 7th (or extension) instead of the triad for each chord.

---

## 2. **Jazzy**

**Chords we choose:** Same as current (I, ii, iii, III, IV, iv, V, vi, vii°).

**Coloring:**
- **I:**   maj7, maj9, 6, add9, 6/9  
- **ii:**  m7, m9  
- **iii:** m7  **or**  maj7, maj9 (III)  
- **IV:**  maj7, maj9, 6  **or**  m7, mMaj7 (iv)  
- **V:**   7, 9, 7sus4, 9sus4  
- **vi:**  m7, m9  
- **vii°:** m7b5  

**Rule:** Higher chance of 7ths/9ths (e.g. 60–70% use extension). Prefer 7ths and 9ths over plain triads.

---

## 3. **Soul / R&B**

**Chords we choose:** I, ii, IV, V, vi (the “strong” degrees; less iii, no vii°).

**Coloring:**
- **I:**   7, maj7, 9, maj9  
- **ii:**  m7, m9  
- **IV:**  7, maj7, 9, 9sus4  
- **V:**   7, 9, 9sus4  
- **vi:**  m7, m9  

**Rule:** Most chords as 7 or 9 (e.g. 70%+). Optional: allow sus4 / 9sus4 on V (and maybe IV) for a soul “lift.”

---

## 4. **Pop / Bright**

**Chords we choose:** I, IV, V, vi (the “four chords”), optionally ii.

**Coloring:**
- **I:**   triad, maj7, add9, 6, 6/9  
- **IV:**  triad, maj7, add9, 6  
- **V:**   triad, 7  
- **vi:**  m, m7, add9  
- **ii:**  m, m7 (if included)  

**Rule:** No dim, no aug, no harsh tensions. 30–50% add an extension (maj7, add9, 6). Keeps everything major/minor and “open.”

---

## 5. **Dark / Moody**

**Chords we choose:** i, iv, v (or V), VI, VII (minor key), or in major: lean on ii, iii, vi, vii° and iv, bVI, bVII.

**Coloring (minor key):**
- **i:**   m, m7, mMaj7  
- **iv:**  m, m7  
- **v or V:**  m7 or 7  
- **VI:**  major, maj7  
- **VII:** major, 7  

**Coloring (if we stay in major but “dark”):**
- **ii, iii, vi:**  m, m7, m9, mMaj7 (for vi or iii)  
- **vii°:**  dim, m7b5  
- **iv (borrowed):**  m, m7, mMaj7  
- **bVI, bVII (borrowed):**  major, 7  

**Rule:** Prefer minor and m7; allow mMaj7 and m7b5 for tension. Few plain major triads on I/IV/V.

---

## 6. **Blues**

**Chords we choose:** Only I, IV, V (three chords).

**Coloring:**
- **I:**   7, 9  
- **IV:**  7, 9  
- **V:**   7, 9  

**Rule:** All dominant 7 (and optionally 9). No maj7, no minor. Can add a “12-bar” pattern: which bar gets I, IV, or V (e.g. I–I–I–I–IV–IV–I–I–V–IV–I–I).

---

## 7. **Latin / Bossa**

**Chords we choose:** I, ii, iii, IV, V, vi (no vii° or rare).

**Coloring:**
- **I:**   maj7, maj9, 6  
- **ii:**  m7, m9  
- **iii:**  m7  **or**  maj7  
- **IV:**  maj7, 6  **or**  m7 (iv)  
- **V:**   7, 9  
- **vi:**  m7, m9  

**Rule:** Almost always 7ths/9ths (80%+). Strong ii–V–I and I–vi–ii–V type moves. Same palette as Jazzy but with more emphasis on these roots.

---

## 8. **Ambient / Pad**

**Chords we choose:** I, ii, iii, IV, V, vi (soft degrees; vii° optional or rare).

**Coloring:**
- **I:**   maj7, add9, 6/9, sus2, 6  
- **ii:**  m7, add9, m9  
- **iii:**  m7, maj7  
- **IV:**  maj7, add9, sus4, 6  
- **V:**   maj7, 7, sus4  
- **vi:**  m7, add9, m9  

**Rule:** No dim, no aug, no 7b9/7#9. Prefer maj7, add9, sus2, sus4, 6, 6/9. “Pretty” colors only.

---

## 9. **Tension / Modern**

**Chords we choose:** All seven degrees (I through vii°), plus optional borrowed (e.g. iv, bVI, bVII).

**Coloring:** Allow “tense” types on the right degrees:
- **I, IV:**  major, maj7, aug (rare)  
- **ii, iii, vi:**  m, m7, m7b5 (on ii/vi), dim (rare)  
- **V:**  7, 9, 7b9, 7#9, 7#11, 7sus4  
- **vii°:**  dim, m7b5, dim7  

**Rule:** 20–30% of chords can be a “tension” color (7b9, 7#9, 7#11, aug, dim, m7b5). Rest diatonic so it still resolves.

---

## 10. **Secondary dominants**

**Chords we choose:** Diatonic chords (I, ii, iii, IV, V, vi, vii°) **plus** one “V of X” before a target chord (e.g. D7 before G, A7 before Dm).

**Which roots:** In C: G = V, D7 = V/V, A7 = V/vi, E7 = V/iii, B7 = V/ii, C7 = V/IV (optional). So we still use the same 12 roots, but some become “dominant 7” leading to the next chord.

**Coloring:**
- **Diatonic chords:**  Same as current (triad, maj7, m7, 7, m7b5, etc. by degree).  
- **Secondary dominant:**  Always **7** or **9** (dominant quality).  

**Rule:** e.g. 1 in 3 or 1 in 4 chords is a secondary dominant that resolves to the next chord (e.g. A7 → Dm).

---

## 11. **Borrowed chords (modal mixture)**

**Chords we choose:** All diatonic (I–vii°) **plus** from parallel minor: **iv**, **bVI**, **bVII** (in C: Fm, Ab, Bb).

**Coloring:**
- **Diatonic:**  Same as current.  
- **iv:**  m, m7, mMaj7  
- **bVI:**  major, maj7  
- **bVII:**  major, 7  

**Rule:** e.g. 1 or 2 chords per progression can be borrowed (iv, bVI, or bVII). Rest stay normal diatonic.

---

## 12. **Extended / Neo-soul**

**Chords we choose:** Same as current (I, ii, iii, IV, V, vi, vii°).

**Coloring:** Allow 9ths and 11ths (and 13ths if we want) on top of 7ths:
- **I:**   maj7, maj9, maj11, maj13  
- **ii:**  m7, m9, m11  
- **iii, IV, vi:**  maj7/m7 as now, plus maj9, m9, add9  
- **V:**  7, 9, 11, 13  
- **vii°:**  m7b5  

**Rule:** Prefer 9ths (and 11ths/13ths where it sounds good). “Big” voicings, same roots.

---

## 13. **Random extensions (same roots)**

**Chords we choose:** Same as current (I, ii, iii, III, IV, iv, V, vi, vii°).

**Coloring:** For **each** degree we **randomly** pick one of several allowed colors:
- **I:**   triad, maj7, add9, 6, 6/9  
- **ii:**  m, m7, m9  
- **iii/III:**  m, m7  **or**  major, maj7  
- **IV/iv:**  major, maj7  **or**  m, mMaj7  
- **V:**   triad, 7, 9  
- **vi:**  m, m7, m9  
- **vii°:**  dim, m7b5  

**Rule:** Each time we pick a chord we randomize the extension (within that list). So same roots, different “colors” every time.

---

## 14. **Minimal**

**Chords we choose:** Only **I, IV, V** (or only **I** and **V**).

**Coloring:**
- **I:**   triad, maj7  
- **IV:**  triad, maj7  
- **V:**   triad, 7  

**Rule:** Very few chords; 20–40% add maj7 or 7. Good for simple, clear progressions.
